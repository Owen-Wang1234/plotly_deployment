Belly Button Biodiversity Dashboard

Over time, people have heard more news about the magnitude of biodiversity that lives on and within the human body. Recently, there have been studies on what sort of life exists inside the human navel. Volunteers have allowed researchers to obtain samples from their navels to see what lives inside. The results have been quite surprising: There are many different species of bacteria living there, and they all vary by species and values for each volunteer.

To better illustrate this, this web page was constructed to chart the biodiversity within the belly button. The dropdown menu allows the user to select a volunteer by the ID number, and the result is a panel containing the demographic information of the volunteer as well as a set of charts displaying the level of biodiverisity and the washing frequency.

Bar Chart - Top 10 OTUs

This chart plots out the top ten bacterial species (identified by their OTU ID) by sample value. When the Test Subject ID is selected from the dropdown menu, the top OTUs (if they exist) found on the volunteer will be charted here. Each bar is labeled with an OTU ID number, and hovering the cursor over a bar will bring up the OTU labels assigned to that specie.

Gauge Chart - Weekly Washing Frequency

This chart plots out how often the volunteer washes the navel per week. When the Test Subject ID is selected from the dropdown menu, the large number will show the frequency (if the volunteer did not give a number, then a "-" will be displayed). The gauge curve will have a black curve that illustrates this value on a scale from 0 to 10. If a "-" was displayed, then the gauge will be blank just like as if "0" was given.

Bubble Chart - Bacterial Cultures Found

This chart plots out all the bacterial species (identified by their OTU ID) and their sample value. When the Test Subject ID is selected from the dropdown menu, each OTU (if one exists) found on the volunteer will be charted here. The OTU ID number determines where on the x-axis the point sits, and the sample value determines both where on the y-axis the point sits and the size of the point. Hovering the cursor over a point will bring up the OTU labels assigned to that specie.
